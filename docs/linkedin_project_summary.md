# LinkedIn Project Content

## Short LinkedIn Caption

I built an ER Wait Time & Healthcare Access Inequality Dashboard using Python, pandas, Streamlit, and Plotly to explore which Central Valley communities may face higher emergency room dependence risk because of lower primary care availability, higher uninsured rates, and socioeconomic access barriers.

## 3-Sentence Project Summary

This portfolio project analyzes healthcare access risk across California Central Valley counties using public healthcare facility data and synthetic county-level demographic indicators. The dashboard ranks communities by modeled ER dependence risk and compares uninsured rate, poverty rate, clinic availability, hospital availability, and synthetic ER visit rates. The goal is to show how healthcare analytics can help public health teams identify communities that may need stronger primary care access, outreach, or resource planning.

## Technical Skills

- Python data cleaning and validation
- pandas data wrangling and feature engineering
- Composite risk scoring and ranking
- Streamlit dashboard development
- Plotly charts and interactive maps
- CSV and Excel export automation
- Public-health analytics storytelling
- Privacy-conscious analytics with no PHI

## Business/Public-Health Value

- Helps identify counties that may rely more heavily on emergency departments due to limited primary care access.
- Supports public health planning by combining insurance, poverty, population, and facility availability indicators.
- Gives healthcare leaders a transparent way to prioritize deeper analysis, community outreach, and resource allocation.
- Demonstrates how privacy-safe analytics can generate useful insights without patient-level data.
